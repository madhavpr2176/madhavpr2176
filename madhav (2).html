About
the writer.

I'm Madhav Pransukhka — a writer, researcher, and student who explores the world through the intersection of stories and structure, the emotion behind numbers and the humanity in ideas.

My work spans economics, sports, and society, blending analytical thinking with creative storytelling. I've contributed to publications like Agora and Arthaniti, and I'm drawn to questions that sit at the intersection of data and human experience.

My writing journey began with sports journalism — 15+ articles on cricket as a reflection of passion, identity, and teamwork. That love for storytelling evolved into policy research and co-founding a newsletter reaching 1,300 readers.

"The most interesting things live between disciplines — where economists have to argue like philosophers, and philosophers have to face the data."
